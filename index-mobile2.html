👨‍⚕️ Physician Assessment — All Patients (Included in Medical Visit Hours)

Every patient receives an initial 1-hour physician assessment plus 3 × 30-minute physician follow-ups over the 2-year program = 2.5 hours of physician time. These are part of the overall medical visit hours and are included across all three models. The physician assessment occurs at intake; follow-ups are typically at months 6, 12, and 18–24.

🧩 ASD Cohort (15% ≈ 60 patients)

Children with Autism spectrum conditions continue to receive the full 25+ hours as individualized 1:1 care across all disciplines, plus the standard 2.5 hrs of physician assessments. These group-based models apply to the remaining ~340 non-ASD patients. The ASD track includes: 2.5 Physician + 5.5 NP + 8 RD + 4 KIN/EP + 4 SW/Psych + 1 Flex = 25 hours.

C

Model C: Block Curriculum + Individual Check-ins

Families move through defined group-based curriculum blocks (6-month phases) interspersed with individual visits. Feels most like a structured "program." New cohorts start every 2–3 months; families who miss a group can attend with the next cohort. Physician assessments are integrated at key transitions.

🗂️ Four Program Phases (tap to expand)

Phase 1: Foundation

Mo 1–6

Healthy eating basics, movement intro, family engagement

👨‍⚕️ Physician: 1× Initial Assessment (60 min)
Groups: 3× Nutrition (90 min) + 1× Activity (60 min)
1:1: 1× NP Intake (60 min) + 1× NP Follow-up (60 min)
Phase Total: 8.5 hrs

Phase 2: Building Skills

Mo 7–12

Meal planning, active living, self-esteem & body image

👨‍⚕️ Physician: 1× Follow-up (30 min)
Groups: 2× Nutrition (90 min) + 1× Activity (60 min) + 1× MH (90 min)
1:1: 1× NP (60 min) + 1× RD (60 min)
Phase Total: 9.0 hrs

Phase 3: Deepening

Mo 13–18

Eating out/social situations, barriers, parent strategies

👨‍⚕️ Physician: 1× Follow-up (30 min)
Groups: 2× Nutrition (90 min) + 1× Activity (60 min) + 1× Parent MH (90 min)
1:1: 1× NP (60 min) + 1× KIN/EP (60 min)
Phase Total: 8.5 hrs

Phase 4: Maintenance

Mo 19–24

Relapse prevention, independence, transition to community

👨‍⚕️ Physician: 1× Follow-up (30 min)
Groups: 1× Nutrition (90 min) + 1× Activity (60 min) + 1× MH (90 min)
1:1: 1× NP (60 min) + 1× RD (60 min) + 1× Graduation NP (60 min)
Phase Total: 6.0 hrs

💡 Key Recommendations (All Models)

👨‍⚕️ Physician Continuity

The 1-hour initial assessment and 3 follow-ups provide consistent physician oversight across all models, ensuring medical complexity is monitored regardless of the care delivery model chosen.

📊 No-Show Buffer

All models intentionally exceed 25 hours. With typical 15–25% no-show rates, scheduling 29–32 hours ensures 25 are actually delivered.

💻 Virtual Group Option

Offering some groups virtually (especially nutrition and parent MH groups) could improve attendance and reduce space constraints.

🧠 Psychology/SW as Consulted

Across all models, Psychology and Social Work contribute primarily through group facilitation and as-needed 1:1 consults, preserving capacity for patients who truly need individualized mental health support.

📝 Note on Group Efficiency

Group sessions multiply effective capacity. A 90-min nutrition group with 10 families delivers 15 patient-hours of care while consuming only 1.5 clinician-hours. This is why even with "surplus" clinician time, the program currently may not be meeting 25 hrs — if everything is 1:1, one clinician produces only one patient-hour per hour worked. Groups are the key multiplier.
