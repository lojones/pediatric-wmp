Groups retain their standard durations (60–90 min). Only the first NP, first MD, and first RD visits are 60 minutes; all other medical/RD follow-ups are 30 minutes. Social Worker and Psychologist visits are always 60 minutes.

🏥 Intake Visit (First Visit — In Person, Integrative Assessment)

Every new patient's first visit is an in-person integrative assessment including:
• NP × 60 min — comprehensive medical assessment
• RD × 60 min — nutrition assessment & initial counseling
• SW or Psych × 60 min — psychosocial screening & family assessment

All three providers see the patient on the same day. Total intake day: 3.0 hours. This sets the foundation for the patient's entire care plan.

👨‍⚕️🩺 Medical Visit Rotation — Physicians & NPs Alternate

Every patient receives 8 medical visits over 2 years (1 every 3 months). The NP leads the intake, then the physician sees the patient at month 3. They alternate after that. Only the first NP visit and first MD visit are 60 minutes; all subsequent medical visits are 30 minutes. Medical visits are always in person and always paired with at least one other resource (usually RD or Ex Phys for bike testing).

💻🏥 Virtual vs In-Person Rules

Always In Person: All medical visits (MD / NP) + their paired resources. Ex Phys bike testing.
Can Be Virtual: RD follow-ups, Kin follow-ups, Ex Phys (non-bike), SW, Psych.
Hospital Rule: A provider cannot see patients virtually in the same morning or afternoon they see patients in person. Virtual and in-person visits must be in separate half-day blocks (AM or PM) or on separate full days.
Bike Testing: Every 3–4 months, in person, usually paired with a medical visit.

🧩 ASD Cohort (15% ≈ 60 patients)

Children with Autism spectrum conditions receive the full 26+ hours as individualized 1:1 care across all disciplines, including the same intake assessment and MD/NP alternating medical visit schedule. Group-based models apply to the remaining ~340 non-ASD patients.

💡 Key Recommendations (All Models)

🏥 Integrative Intake Assessment

All models begin with the same 3-hour intake day (NP 60 + RD 60 + SW/Psych 60). This provides comprehensive baseline assessment and allows the team to identify needs early, including tiering decisions for Model B.

🔗 Always Pair Medical Visits

Medical visits are always in person and always paired with at least one other resource (usually RD or Ex Phys for bike testing). This maximizes the value of each in-person trip and ensures coordinated care.

💻 Virtual When Possible

RD, Kin, Ex Phys (non-bike), SW, and Psych can all see patients virtually for follow-up 1:1 visits. This improves access and reduces travel burden. Remember: virtual and in-person must be in separate half-day blocks per hospital rules.

🚴 Bike Testing Cadence

Exercise Physiology bike testing every 3–4 months, paired with in-person medical visits. ~5–6 bike tests per patient over 2 years. Ex Phys can do virtual follow-ups on non-bike-test days.

🔵🟢🟡 Model-Specific Schedule Adjustments

🔵 Model A (Group-Heavy)

  • More group slots needed: Add 1 extra nutrition group slot per week (Wed PM + Fri PM rotation)
  • Fewer interim 1:1 slots: ~4 interim pairs per patient over 2 yrs → lower 1:1 demand
  • Virtual blocks stay light: Most care hours come from groups and paired medical visits
  • Group schedule: Nutrition groups Wed PM (weeks 1,3) & Fri PM (weeks 2,4). Activity groups Tue AM. MH groups Thu PM monthly.

🟢 Model B (Hybrid Tiered)

  • Variable 1:1 demand: Tier 3 patients need 6+ interim visit pairs + 3 Psych/SW sessions → schedule more virtual blocks
  • Psych/SW 1:1 demand highest: Tier 2 (1 session) + Tier 3 (3 sessions). Dedicate Psych 1 Mon/Tue for Tier 3, Psych 2 Thu/Fri for Tier 2.
  • Fewer groups than Model A: Tier 3 has fewest groups. Can run fewer group sessions overall.
  • Intake includes tiering assessment: SW/Psych at intake uses standardized tool to assign tier.

🟡 Model C (Block Curriculum)

  • Fixed group calendar: Nutrition groups every 2–3 weeks, Activity monthly, MH quarterly per cohort.
  • Cohort management: New cohort every 2–3 months. Multiple cohorts running simultaneously in different phases.
  • Group slots most predictable: Wed PM nutrition, Fri PM activity (rotating), Thu PM MH.
  • Phase-based curriculum: Clinicians rotate through defined content. Phase 1 = Foundation topics, Phase 4 = Maintenance/transition.
  • Catch-up mechanism: Missed a group? Join the next cohort's corresponding session.
