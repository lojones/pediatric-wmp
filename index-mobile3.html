👨‍⚕️🩺 Medical Visit Rotation — Physicians & NPs Alternate

Every patient receives 8 medical visits over 2 years (1 every 3 months). Physicians and NPs alternate these visits: the physician sees each patient ~2× per year (4 over 2 years) and the NP sees them for the other 4 visits. There are 3 physicians who rotate through the clinic; one physician is available at any given time. This shared model reduces NP burden while ensuring physician oversight at regular intervals.

C

Model C: Block Curriculum + Individual Check-ins

Families move through defined group-based curriculum blocks (6-month phases) with MD/NP visits alternating at phase transitions. New cohorts start every 2–3 months.

🗂️ Four Program Phases (tap to expand)

Phase 1: Foundation

Mo 1–6

Healthy eating basics, movement intro, family engagement

Medical: 1× MD (60 min) + 1× NP (60 min)
Groups: 3× Nutrition (90 min) + 1× Activity (60 min)

Mo 7–12

💡 Key Recommendations (All Models)

👨‍⚕️🩺 MD/NP Alternation

The alternating physician/NP model ensures every patient has regular physician oversight (2×/year) while keeping NP workload manageable. With 3 rotating physicians, each sees ~133 patients/year. Handoff notes between MD and NP visits are critical for continuity.

📊 No-Show Buffer

All models intentionally exceed 25 hours. With typical 15–25% no-show rates, scheduling 27–30 hours ensures 25 are actually delivered.

💻 Virtual Group Option

Offering some groups virtually (especially nutrition and parent MH groups) could improve attendance and reduce space constraints.

🧠 Psychology/SW as Consulted

Across all models, Psychology and Social Work contribute primarily through group facilitation and as-needed 1:1 consults.

📈 Tracking Dashboard

A simple dashboard tracking cumulative care hours per patient helps identify anyone falling behind early enough to intervene.
